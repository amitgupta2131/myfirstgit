About Us

A State's foremost priority is to look after the welfare of its people. Being an agrarian State, UP Government's foremost priority is to look after the welfare of its farmers. In protecting the interests of its 4 million sugarcane farmers, the Government ensures that the farmers get a good sum of money for their hard toil, every year, from the sugar millers who purchase cane for the production of sugar. The price which the State determines as the purchase price of the sugarcane is known as SAP. It is much higher than the FRP, which the Centre decides as the price of sugarcane every year.
Though SAP is increasing the total acreage under sugarcane, but it is not augmenting yields per hectare or improving sugarcane recovery rate, which continues to be lowest in UP compared to other States producing sugar. Yield improvement and better recovery rates should be the two foremost priorities of the Government, which will be in the interest of all the stake-holders including the sugar consumers who are otherwise exposed to a high cost sugarcane and sugar.
A farmer in UP earns more than Rs 80,000 per hectare from sugarcane farming, whereas wheat and paddy crop combination from the same patch of land fetches the farmer almost half the price in a year. This defmitely makes sugarcane farming very remunerative as compared to other crops.
Sugarcane is cultivated on 24 lakh hectares of land which is about 8.56% of the total gross cropped area. The average yield of sugarcane in UP is 62 tons per hectare. The total acreage under sugarcane has gone up by 15% in the last five years, but the yield and recovery percentage hasn't kept pace with it. SAP is also creating crop imbalances and the rejected variety of sugarcane are finding an easy way to the sugar mills as it fetches a good price of 275 per quintal.
Moreover, if the area under sugarcane cultivation keeps increasing every year, it will shrink the cultivation area of other important crops that are produced in UP, mainly of wheat and paddy. A lopsided foodgrain production would create huge imbalances in the foodgrain basket of the country. This could also result in nutrient imbalances in the soil, ground water imbalances, etc. and eventually will render the soil untillable for sugarcane.
A high SAP is also unfair to the 119 sugar mills operating in the State presently. They have to bear an even higher financial obligation as the cost of production rises every year due to the steep increase in SAP. Also the ex-mill price of sugar is not good enough to recover losses and make profit. This deepens the financial mess of the sugar mills who eventually are unable to make timely payment to the farmers and lead to increase in cane arrears. .Fisst and foremost, cane price has to be necessarily linked to total yield and recovery percentage of sugar rather than just the weight or quantity of the sugarcane. Additionally, to stop the cropping imbalance, farmers should be promoted to take up inter cropping programme on large scale.
This will increase the income during mid-season. Sugarcane price should be decided in tandem with other crops, so that other crops also look equally remunerative. Sugarcane crop balance has to be improved like early: mid: late crop in the ratio of 20-15%, 40-45% and 40-45% respectively for better results. Management practice of ratoon, which contributes about 50% of the total area in Uttar Pradesh, should be improved to enhance yield. The Government should also give sops to the industry to invest in Research and Development for developing high yield crops and better sowing techniques. The sugarcane farmers and the sugar industry should be made more competitive on its own to brace any headwinds.

Uttar Pradesh Sugar Industry

1. Employ 2.5 lac people directly in 122 operational sugar mills and distilleries out of total 158 installed sugar mills. Thereby supports about 13 lac persons directly, including their family members.

2. Provides revenue to state exchequer of Rs. 18,000 crore --- Rs. 6,000 cr directly thru local and state taxes --- Rs. 12,000 cr indirectly thru Liquor Sale (Excise Duty of Liquor)

3. Over 50 Lac farmers directly grow sugarcane. Considering a household of 5 persons per farmer, 2.5 crore or about 15% of the population of the state earn their livelihood directly thru sugarcane.

4. Sugarcane is grown in 44 districts of the state i.e. roughly half of the 75 districts of UP, which takes care of direct rural development activity in these 44 districts.

5. Cane is the largest cash crop in the State.

6. Sugar Industry generates revenues of Rs. 30,000 crore annually, making it the largest industry of UP.
